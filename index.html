VOLLEYBALL

Volleyball is a team sport in which two teams of six players are separated by a net. Each team tries to score points by grounding a ball on the other team's court under organized rules. It has been a part of the official program of the Summer Olympic Games since Tokyo 1964.

KABBADI

Kabaddi is a high-intensity, contact team sport originating in ancient India. Two teams of seven players take turns sending an offensive player, called a "raider," into the opposing half of the court to tag defenders, returning to their side in a single breath while continuously chanting "Kabaddi".

CRICKET

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of 11 players on an oval field. The batting team scores "runs" by hitting a bowled ball and swapping positions between two wickets, or by hitting it past the boundary. The fielding team aims to dismiss the batters and prevent runs.

KHO-KHO

Kho Kho is a traditional Indian tag sport dating back thousands of years. Played on a rectangular court, the game requires two teams of 12 players (9 on the field) alternating between chasing and evading. The chasing team sits in a line and passes a "Kho" (touch) to tag defenders as fast as possible.
